Notification vs. Disclosure — Fund I LP

German change-of-control regime (§ 2c KWG). Cell value = the LP’s look-through holding in the bank = (LP % of Fund I) × (Fund I % of bank).

€800M

An LP ticket is a fixed € amount; its % of Fund I falls as the fund grows. The bank is valued at €2bn (100% = €2bn). Increasing Fund I dilutes every LP and pushes cells toward disclosure / no requirement.

Columns: Fund I’s % of the bank  ·  Rows: LP ticket (and resulting % of Fund I)

Row labels recompute live with the Fund I size above.

Notification required — look-through ≥ 10% (own § 2c filing)
Disclosure only — 0.5% < look-through < 10% (named in structure chart)
No requirement — look-through ≤ 0.5%
Reading the green cells: the tag shows which further thresholds are crossed — ≥20% / ≥30% / ≥50% are separate notification points under § 2c KWG.
Control overlay (not shown): if the LP’s stake gives it control of Fund I (majority votes, veto/consent over investments, board/appointment rights), the multiplication result is replaced by full attribution of Fund I’s entire bank stake to the LP — e.g. the whole 40% column collapses to a 40% look-through regardless of the LP %.
Assumptions: single fund branch, LP not acting in concert with others, the bank is a CRR credit institution (ECB decides on BaFin’s draft). This is an illustrative tool, not legal advice.
